The Smart Side

Smart where
you least expected.

Inside, Gira has a microcontroller that does what no classic record player does: precise control from your phone, software calibration, and customizable operating modes.

Web Interface

Control from the browser.
No app. No cloud.

Connect to the local Wi-Fi and control everything straight from your phone's browser. Turn on the motor, adjust rotation, raise and lower the tonearm. All offline, all yours.

Silent Rotation

33⅓ & 45 RPM
via TMC2209.

NEMA17 stepper motor controlled in microstepping with StealthChop2. Practically inaudible. Smooth acceleration and deceleration ramps.

33⅓
45

Magnetic Sensor

Absolute angular reading.

MT6701 sensor monitors the tonearm angle in real time via I²C. Gira detects the start of the record, the end of the side — and never scratches the vinyl.

107° current position

Modes

Manual or Automatic.

In automatic mode, the lift lowers the stylus after the debounce time, plays the entire side, and raises the tonearm when the end is detected. In manual mode, you're in control.

Manual
Auto

Wi-Fi & OTA

Self-configuring.
Updates over the air.

On first boot, Gira creates the TocaDiscos_Setup network and opens a captive portal for you to enter your Wi-Fi. Afterwards, firmware updates via OTA — no cable needed.

Anti-Skating

Lateral force compensation.
Stylus always centered.

A counterweight system with micro-adjustments calibrates anti-skating compensation, keeping the stylus centered in the vinyl groove and reducing uneven cartridge wear.

0 compensation

Anatomy

Three layers.
One record player.

Everything you need to build your Gira is divided into three fronts — and each one of them is 100% open.

3D Printing

.stl files ready to print.

  • Modular chassis, base, and tonearm
  • Printed gear system
  • Compatible with standard FDM printers (200×200mm)
  • Suggested filament: PLA or PETG
Download .STL Files

Electronics

Modern stack, accessible components.

  • Seeed Studio XIAO ESP32-C3 (native Wi-Fi)
  • TMC2209 driver — UART communication
  • NEMA17 motor for the platter
  • MT6701 magnetic sensor (I²C) for the tonearm
  • Micro servo for tonearm lift
View connection diagram →

Firmware

Open source. Updatable. Hackable.

  • PlatformIO + Arduino framework
  • Embedded PWA via LittleFS
  • WebSockets for real-time telemetry
  • Access at tocadiscos.local via mDNS
  • OTA, Telnet debug, and NVS persistence
